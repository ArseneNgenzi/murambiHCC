Who We Are

Summary

SERUKA is a national Non-Governmental Organization founded by 173 women in 1992 with an aim and concern to fight Gender Based violence (GBV) and develop a woman economically, psychologically and culturally through promoting girls’ education and national political involvement. SERUKA was granted legal Personality by the Ministerial Order n° 174/05 of 22nd May, 1992 which was revised by the Ministerial Order n° 93/11 of 14th September 2005 (OG n°22 of 15/11/2008). SERUKA Organization is currently recognized and registered with the Rwanda Governance Board (RGB) by the Official Gazette n°49 of 5/12/2016.

SERUKA enjoys strong networking with government institutions including all districts of operations, ministry of local government; Gender and Family Promotion; Agriculture and Animal Resources, Rwanda Agriculture Board; Rwanda Cooperative Agency, Rwanda Governance Board and with many international and national NGOs. SERUKA produced country leaders at different levels including one of the major Founders, the late Prime Mister Uwiringiyimana Agathe (killed during the 1994 Tutsi genocide). SERUKA is a founder member of women’s umbrella organization, Profemmes Twesehamwe (PFTH) SERUKA, an active member of Conseil de Concertation des Organizations d’Appui aux Initaiatives de Base (CCOIAB), an Umbrella for Civil society Organisations in Rwanda and also a member of Reseau Femmes Africaines pour le Développement Durable (REFADD).

Mission

SERUKA mission focuses on strengthening key areas to be implemented to ensure a robust, coherent, coordinated and comprehensive response to GBV, build confidence, raise self-esteem and promote women and girls social, educational economic and political development

Vision

SERUKA strives to create a future in which all women will be viewed and treated equally as men, in all aspects of life: civil, political, economic, social, and cultural. Our vision is that all women become self-sustaining and remain well recognized in their community and truly empowered

Objectives
Main Objectives

SERUKA aims at Women’s social economic promotion through promotion of girl education and fight against Gender Based Violence(GBV)

Specific Objectives

  • To create an awareness and better understanding among individuals, public and relevant agencies on the issues of violence against women and the underlying inequalities
  • To fight malnutrition among families mainly the children and pregnant women
  • To prevent early pregnancies of girls in schools which is a dominant reason for school drop-outs
  • To raise confidence among girls who faced early pregnancies to fight back and develop themselves back to the economic peak
  • To undertake and encourage research into any of the factors that contribute to the inequality and subordination of women
  • To promote economic development of women and self-sustaining
  • To promote education of girls and build their confidence in contribution of the development of the country politically, economically and culturally
  • To Develop and promote educational training and action programs for women, especially the under-privileged
  • To undertake and advocate under the partnership of other non-government organization the eradication of factors that contribute to inequality and subordination of women
